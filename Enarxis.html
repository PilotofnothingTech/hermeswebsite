Rocket design

Simple, modular architecture prioritizing reliability over complexity.

Airframe

  • Structure: 3D‑printed ABS+ body tubes and bulkheads with bayonet twist‑lock interfaces.
  • Fins: ABS+ fins bonded to the motor segment with cyanoacrylate; alignment via printed mounts.
  • Rail interfaces: Dual rail buttons placed on water and electronics segments for stable launch guidance.
  • Payload: ~216 ml water bladder constrained with a compression spacer to minimize slosh.

Avionics & Comms

  • Arduino Nano 33 BLE Sense Rev2 (IMU + MCU)
  • SD data logging
  • SX1278 LoRa — 433 MHz telemetry (≈30 Hz)
  • 2S Li‑ion battery, 3.3 V regulator, buzzer w/ transistor driver
  • Ground station: SX1278 + Raspberry Pi Pico W bridge, high‑gain loaded antenna

Propulsion & Stability (sim)

  • Motor: commercial solid rocket motor (CRC‑class)
  • Apogee (pred.): ~691 m
  • Max accel: ~65 m/s²
  • Stability at rail departure: ~0.52 cal; Max stability ~2.73 cal

Values from OpenRocket‑style simulation and team estimates.

Parachute — analysis & design

Chosen for high drag per area, compact packing, and stable descent.

Concept

  • Type: Toroidal (annular) canopy, central vent for stability
  • Why: Higher drag coefficient (~2.0–2.2) vs. hemispherical chutes (~1.0–1.5), smaller pack volume
  • Deployment: Spring‑loaded piston; servo‑latched pin releases at apogee
  • Backup: Trigger deploy if no apogee detected after ~15 s

Materials & build

  • Light nylon canopy (≈50–60 g/m²), low porosity
  • Ring‑shaped gores; double‑stitched (flat‑fell seams)
  • 8 suspension lines (mono fishing line ≈0.5 mm) tied to inner & outer rims; central swivel
  • Line length ≈1.5× canopy diameter; reinforced attachment patches

CFD & tests (summary)

  • CFD: Incompressible flow, k‑ε; target descent ~7 m/s; predicted drag ≈11 N (Cd≈2.0–2.2)
  • Drop tests: 3‑story building with 1 kg payload; full inflation ≈1 s
  • Terminal velocity: ~5–6 m/s (video frame analysis, 240 fps)
  • Opening loads: modest; no payload damage (incl. egg‑drop demo)

Deployment device: compressed spring + piston plate; servo unlatches at apogee.

Manufacturing

Process

  • ABS+ parts 3D‑printed on Bambu Labs X1C and Creality K1C
  • Typical settings: up to 200 mm/s, 260 °C nozzle; bulkheads at ~85% infill (cubic)
  • Post‑processing: light sanding, fit checks; cyanoacrylate bonding for fins

Assembly highlights

  • Segmented architecture: motor → water bay → electronics → nose
  • Connector rings with captive nuts; bayonet locks on avionics bulkheads
  • Water payload constrained with spacer to limit slosh
  • Dual rail buttons aligned across segments

Rocket analysis & testing

Sim results

  • Apogee (pred.): ~691 m
  • Max acceleration: ~65.1 m/s²
  • Stability: ~0.52 cal at rail departure; max ~2.73 cal
  • Descent speed target: ~6.3 m/s

Guides & procedures

  • Pre‑flight: technical inspection, scoring committee, parts layout
  • Stepwise assembly from motor segment upward; electronics/altimeter arming sequence
  • Ground station bring‑up; telemetry link check (LoRa 433 MHz)
  • Flight ops: launch, telemetry monitoring, recovery, cleanup, results

Recovery testing

  • Spring‑ejection reliability validated in repeated ground tests
  • Drop tests confirm fast inflation and stable descent
  • Backup deploy logic: trigger after ~15 s if no apogee detection

Video analysis at 240 fps used to estimate velocity/acceleration profiles.
